Central clearing and risk transformation

  • Background of the CCP
    • Functions of the CCPs
      • Central clearing for OTC
      • Collateral requirements
    • Benefits of CCPs
      • CCP becomes counterparty to both sides
      • isolate default
      • Initial margin and variation margin
      • Multilatieral netting
      • Reduce OP risk
  • Realized Losses vs Accounting Losses
    • Accounting Losses and Realized losses
    • Insolvent
      • asset < liabilities
      • Capital requirements address the issue of solvency
    • Illiquid
      • Fails to meet a short-term payment obligation
  • How Margin Requirements Transform Counterparty Risk into Liquidity Risk
    • Margin Requirements
      • Initial, variation margin
      • Default fund
    • Margin’s Impact on the Balance Sheet
      • Posting initial margin has little impact
      • Default Fund contributions: small impact
      • Variation margin: impact the liquidity reserves
      • CCP: replace counterparty risk by liquidity risk
  • CCPs As Liquidity Intermediaries
    • CCPs’ Capital
      • CCPs’ capital is so small.
    • CCPs’ Loss Absorption Role
      • A CCP is affected only when clearing members default
      • Liquidity risk to the CCP, not an insolvency risk
  • Provisioning for Default Losses: The CCP Loss Waterfall
    • Initial Margin
      • Cannot offset losses of other members
    • Default fund contribution of defaulting member
    • Mutualisation of large losses
    • Recovery
      • An additional contribution by non-defaulting clearing members
    • Failure Resolution
  • Margin Requirements Should Address Liquidation Costs
    • Liquidation Cost
      • Loss between the default time and the liquidation
      • Proportional to the gross notional size
    • Accounting for Liquidation Costs
      • Unwinding of positions whose magnitude is large require more time
      • Margin requirements need to include a liquidity charge
    • Benefits of Liquidation Costs
      • Deter members from accumulating concentrated exposures
    • Application of Liquidation Costs to the Default Fund
      • Toward those large clearing members
      • Their defaults: widening of bid-ask spreads and liquidation costs
  • Liquidity at the End of the Waterfall: Recovery Mechanisms for CCPs
    • Recovery Arrangements
      • Reach the end of the waterfall
    • Default Fund Assessments
      • Clearing members add an additional contribution
      • Non-defaulted members may fail to have enough liquidity
  • Variation Margin Haircuts
    • Allocates losses across surviving members while providing for continity of clearing services
    • VMGH restores the liquidity resources of the CCP at the expenses of clearing members.

What is SOFR?

  • Enter SOFR
    • Secured Overnight Financing Rate (SOFR)
      • Definition
      • Publication
      • Composition
      • The transaction-weighted median repo rate becomes the day’s SOFR benchmark value
    • Three Advantages
      • The median is more robust
      • Actually observed data
  • How Do SOFR and EFFR Compare?
    • Important Differences
      • Daily SOFR is lower than daily EFFR
      • SOFR does not drop on the last business day of each month
      • SOFR is more volatile
  • CME SOFR Futures
  • One-Month SOFR and One-Month EFFR
    • Comparative Features
      • The SOFR-EFFR spread varies in relation to monetary policy
  • Three-Month SOFR, Three-Month EFFR, and Three-Month ICE LIBOR
    • The 3-month EFFR and 3-month SOFR estimates are perfect foresight rates
    • 3-month ICE LIBOR is a bona fide market rate
    • Comparisons
      • The spread varies with the tone of US monetary policy.
