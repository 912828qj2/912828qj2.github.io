Factor Theory

  • Factors and CAPM
    • Factors definition
      • Factors are to assets what nutrients are to food.
      • Factor risks are driving force behind assets’ risk premiums.
      • Factors matter, not assets.
      • Assets are bundles of factors.
      • Different investors need different risk factors.
    • Factor risks are bad.
    • Factor theory specifies different types of underlying factor risk.
    • CAPM and its assumptions
      • Assumptions:
        1. No transaction costs
        2. Assets are infinitely divisible
        3. The absence of personal income tax.
        4. An individual can not affect the price of a stock by his trading.
        5. Investors’ decision making solely depend on terms of returns and standard deviations of the returns.
        6. Unlimited short sales are allowed.
        7. Unlimited lending and borrowing at the riskless rate.
        8. All investors have identical expectations.
        9. All Assets are marketable.
      • CAPM risk premiums depend only on the assets’ beta, which means only one factor-market portfolio.
      • Shortcomings of CAPM come from its assumptions.
      • Basic intuitions of the CAPM still holds true: risk premiums are compensation for investors’ losses during bad times.

Factors

  • Factors
    • Factors Investing
      • Value Investin and Value Premium
      • A value-growth strategy is long value stocks and short growth stocks.
      • A value stock has a high book-to-market ratio. A growth stock has a low book-t-market ratio.
    • Macroeconomic risk factors
      • Economic growth, inflation, and volatility are the three most important macro factors that affect asset prices.
      • Rather than level of a factor, it is the unanticipated change to a risk factor that affects asset prices.
    • Mitigating volatility risk and Challenges
      • Two Baisc approaches to mitigate volatility risk:
        1. Invest in less-volatile assets like bonds.
        2. Buy volatility protection in the derivatives market.
    • Dynamic risk factor
      • The Fama-French model explains asset returns on three dynamic facotrs:
        1. Traditional CAPM market risk factor
        2. A factor that captures size effect (SMB or small cap minus big cap)
        3. A factor that captures value/growth effect (HML or high book-to-market value minus low book-to-market value)
    • Value and momentum investment strategies
      • A momentum strategy is long “winners” and short “losers”.
      • Value strategy is a negative feedback strategy, momentum is a positive feedback strategy.
      • Momentum strategies are subject to crashes.
    • Different strategy based on different situations and needs.
